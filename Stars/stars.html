Neutron Star

Neutron stars are small but incredibly dense. They are the first step down from black holes.

White Dwarf

As the remains of a supernova, white drwarfs are small, low energy star that will eventually burn out into a black dwarf.

Black Dwarf

The remains of a white dwarf. It has no energy, besides gravity. It is completely black, and has never been seen before.

Red Giant

An expanding visibly red star that expands because of temperatures caused by the contracting of the star due to gravity.

Supergiant

Massive stars.

Black Hole

An extremely dense collapsed supernova. In such gravity, not even light can escape (makes it black).

Yellow

Medium temperature stars in their main sequence. Much like our sun.

Protostar

A large collection of hydrogen, helium, and other interstellar medium gases that has achieved nuclear fusion.
